# Emergency State Flag – Concept 4: The Trust Grid

**Description**
- Field: Solid medium gray (#777777 or Pantone Cool Gray 11 C).
- Charge: A fine white grid (1‑unit squares, line weight ~2% of hoist) overlaying the entire field.
- At the exact center of the flag, the grid lines break to form a small open circle (diameter ~1/5 of the hoist), suggesting a lens, an eye, or a point of focus where data meets human judgment.

**Symbolism**
- The gray field represents neutrality, verification, and the move from noise to signal—neither the black of opposition nor the white of passivity.
- The white grid stands for measurement, transparency, and the principle of “no secrets”: everything is laid out, observable, and verifiable.
- The broken grid forming an open circle at the center highlights the human element—trust, discernment, and the responsibility to act on verified information. It is the point where the emergency state’s core verbs converge: **Verify. Don’t assume. No secrets. Humanitarian priority. Built for trust.**

**Usage Notes**
- Recommended ratio (hoist:fly) = 2:3 for versatility.
- The design scales well: the grid can be rendered as thin lines for small flags or as a more pronounced reticle for large banners.
- If a single accent color is desired to emphasize humanitarian priority, a thin red (#ED1C24 or Pantone Warm Red C) rim around the open circle may be added, but the pure grayscale version already conveys the intended message.
