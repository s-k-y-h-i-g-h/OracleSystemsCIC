## 1. Charity Name

**self++ Foundation**

---

## 2. Mission Statement

To promote and advance human longevity for the public benefit by:
- funding and supporting longevity research
- making longevity treatments, protocols, and supplements accessible to people in poverty
- removing financial and informational barriers to evidence-based life extension

---

## 3. Charity Objects (Legal Purposes)

The charity's objects are:

1. **Research funding** — to advance the study of interventions that extend healthy human lifespan, with a focus on treatments accessible to underserved populations

2. **Public education** — to provide free, evidence-based information about longevity, biohacking, and life extension through open-access publications, data platforms, and community resources

3. **Accessibility** — to distribute supplements, protocols, and health interventions to individuals who cannot afford them, particularly those in financial hardship

4. **Advocacy** — to campaign for regulatory reform that accelerates affordable access to longevity treatments (e.g., MHRA/FDA approval pathways)

---

## 4. Governance Structure

### Type
CIO (Charitable Incorporated Organisation) — structure preferred for its simplicity vs a full charitable trust.

### Trustees
- **George Zankevich** (founder trustee)
- 2 additional trustees to be confirmed
- Decisions made by majority vote
- Founder (George Zankevich) retains veto on mission-critical decisions for first 5 years

### Operations
- Day-to-day management delegated to founder/director
- Trustee board meets quarterly
- Annual public impact report published open-source

## 5. Financial Basis

### Initial Funding
- Founder personal contribution (nominal)
- Grants from aligned foundations (future)
- Donations via website (future)

### Funds Usage
- Research grants
- Supplement distribution programmes
- Platform hosting and content costs
- Administrative costs (target: <10% of annual spend)

## 6. Registration Checklist

- [ ] Confirm charity name is available (Charity Commission name check)
- [ ] Draft and approve these objects/clauses
- [ ] Identify minimum 3 trustees (including founder)
- [ ] Create trustee undertaking agreements
- [ ] Prepare financial forecasts (even basic)
- [ ] Complete Charity Commission online application
- [ ] Pay registration fee (£8 online)
- [ ] Submit and note reference number
- [ ] Await Charity Commission review (~40 days)
- [ ] Receive registration confirmation
- [ ] Set up bank account in charity name
- [ ] Publish annual return on Charity Commission website

## 7. Notes

- The existing [Charity repo](https://github.com/s-k-y-h-i-g-h/Charity) on GitHub serves as the public-facing content hub — this aligns with the "public education" object
- The CIC (Community Interest Company) will sit alongside the charity as a commercial vehicle — charity owns/controls it
- CIO rather than charitable trust chosen for: limited liability for trustees, simpler reporting, clearer regulatory path
